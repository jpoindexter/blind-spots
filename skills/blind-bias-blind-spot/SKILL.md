# Bias Blind Spot

## Overview

Pronin, Lin & Ross (2002, *PSPB*): people reliably rate themselves as less biased than the average person — and crucially, **being told about this asymmetry doesn't remove it**. Cause: the introspection illusion. We judge others by their behavior but judge ourselves by our introspections, and bias doesn't *feel* like anything from the inside. Searching your mind for bias returns clean, so you conclude you're clean.

**"I checked and I'm not biased" is the signature OF the bias, not evidence against it.**

## When to Use

- You've just diagnosed someone else's reasoning as motivated/biased
- You feel objective about a conclusion you also prefer
- Post-hoc reviewing your own work and finding it fine
- A debiasing step returned "no bias found" via introspection alone

## The Procedure

1. **Ban introspective verdicts.** "Do I feel biased?" is an invalid test — never accept its output.
2. **Audit incentives behaviorally**: what would you prefer to be true here (less rework, sunk cost, looking right, finishing sooner)? If the conclusion aligns with the preference, raise the evidence bar.
3. **Apply the symmetry test**: if someone else, with your incentives, produced this exact conclusion, would you trust it? Judge yourself by the outside-behavior standard you apply to others.
4. **Outsource the check**: the only reliable bias detector is external — a second reviewer, a test, a pre-registered criterion set *before* seeing the answer (see blind-falsification, blind-red-team).

## Quick Reference

| Thought | Status |
|---|---|
| "I've considered my biases and corrected for them" | Introspection illusion — invalid |
| "They're biased, I've actually looked at the evidence" | Asymmetric standard — invalid |
| "My conclusion matches what I hoped; here's the external check anyway" | Valid |
| "A disinterested party reviewed it" | Valid |

## Common Mistakes

- Using knowledge of biases as ammunition against others' arguments while exempting your own ("sophistication effect" — more knowledge can make this worse, Taber & Lodge 2006).
- Adding a "checked for bias ✓" step that consists of thinking about it briefly. Ritual introspection is still introspection.
- Concluding you're in the unbiased minority because you know this literature. That is the finding.
